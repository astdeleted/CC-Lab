Spike Jelly

Scientific Name:

Quadraspike Scyphozoa

Discovered by / at:

Brian Sun, August 21, 2077

Family:

Although these creatures look different, they belong to the same family that jellyfish belong to.

Habitat:

Spike jellies like to live around the ocean floor. They float around for majority of their life. They like to move around in sea grass, occasionally eating the grass.

Appearance:

They look like blobs of jelly. A spike jellies shape changes often and they are never the same shape twice. They are often found in a deep shade of green. Once agitated, they will harden their structure, turning into a sphere with four spikes protruding out of them. Spike jellies will also appear bioluminescent when agitated.

Key Characteristics:

  • Jelly-like bodies
  • Lives near the ocean floor
  • Likes hiding in dark crevices
  • Bioluminescent bodies
  • Extremely unpredicatble when provoked

What we know so far about Spike Jellies?

Gelatinous creatures that float around near deep ocean floors. They belong to the jellyfish family. Their bodies have adapted to the high pressure environment. They float around mindlessly until provoked by something. They are highly unpredictable when provoked; they move around frantically and deploy four large spikes that spin rapidly.

Guidelines for human interaction with this creature

It is not recommended to interact with this creature without proper equipment.

Move your mouse near the creature for it to enter the agitated state. The spike jelly will try to get away from the mouse as quickly as it can. You can continue to chase the creature by following it with your cursor.
